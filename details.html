What is Treaty 1?

  • Treaty 1 was made in 1871.
  • It was an agreement between the Canadian government and several First Nations.
  • The First Nations lived in Manitoba for many years before Europeans arrived.

Who Was Involved in Treaty 1?

  • Seven First Nations groups signed Treaty 1:
    • Brokenhead
    • Sagkeeng
    • Long Plain
    • Peguis
    • Roseau River
    • Sandy Bay
    • Swan Lake
  • These First Nations are part of the Anishinaabe (Ojibway) and Cree peoples.

Why Was Treaty 1 Made?

  • The Canadian government wanted to use the land for farms and towns.
  • In exchange, they promised to help the First Nations by:
    • Giving them land to live on (called reserves).
    • Providing them with money every year.
    • Helping them farm by giving tools, animals, and seeds.
    • Allowing them to hunt and fish on the land.

What Did the First Nations Think?

  • The First Nations thought they were sharing the land with settlers.
  • The Canadian government thought they were taking the land.
  • This caused problems because the First Nations didn't get everything they were promised.

Why is Treaty 1 Important?

  • Treaty 1 shows how people tried to work together.
  • Today, First Nations are still working with the government to ensure the promises of Treaty 1 are kept.
  • Many places where people live today, like Winnipeg, are on Treaty 1 land.

The Crown, Anishinaabe, Swampy Cree, and Canada's First Nations communities are involved in the Treaty 1 territory.
